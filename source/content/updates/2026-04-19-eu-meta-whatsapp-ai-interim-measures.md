## EC Issues Supplementary Charge Sheet Against Meta Over WhatsApp AI Rival Exclusion — Interim Measures Imminent

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: iFood, Swiggy, PayU — all use WhatsApp Business API for customer communications; broader relevance as AI assistant market competition precedent
- **Priority**: CRITICAL
- **Source**: [European Commission / European Sting](https://europeansting.com/2026/04/16/commission-sends-meta-fresh-charge-sheet-on-possible-interim-measures-to-reverse-exclusion-of-third-party-ai-assistants-from-whatsapp/)

On 15 April 2026 the European Commission escalated its antitrust case against Meta by issuing a Supplementary Statement of Objections over WhatsApp's exclusion of third-party AI assistants. The Commission found that Meta's revised March 2026 pricing framework for third-party AI providers — requiring fees that render integration commercially unviable — has the same exclusionary effect as the outright access ban Meta imposed in October 2025. The Commission intends to impose interim measures ordering Meta to restore third-party AI assistant access under pre-October 2025 conditions while the full Article 102 TFEU investigation continues. The investigation was formally opened in December 2025, with a first Statement of Objections sent in February 2026; this is the second charge sheet. This is a landmark moment for AI market competition: it is the first time the Commission has sought interim measures to protect competition in the AI assistant market specifically. For Prosus portfolio companies that integrate or compete with AI on messaging infrastructure — and for any business in the WhatsApp Business API ecosystem — the outcome will set the terms of AI distribution through the world's largest messaging platform.
