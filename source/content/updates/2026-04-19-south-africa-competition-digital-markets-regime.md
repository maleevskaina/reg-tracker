## South Africa Launches Formal Digital Markets Regime via Competition Law

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: South Africa
- **Company relevance**: OLX (South Africa classifieds operations), Prosus (primary listing on JSE/Euronext)
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [Werksmans Attorneys](https://werksmans.com/south-africas-digital-markets-regime-has-arrived-and-it-lives-inside-competition-law/)

South Africa's Competition Commission has moved decisively into digital markets regulation. The Commission released the final Media and Digital Platforms Market Inquiry (MDPMI) report in November 2025, following a multi-year investigation into how Google and Meta have concentrated power in search, social media, and digital advertising — at the expense of local journalism. In February 2026, the Commission gazetted its Online Intermediation Platforms (OIP) Guidance Note, setting out categories of platform conduct likely to attract scrutiny including self-preferencing, data access restrictions, and unfair ranking. In March 2026, it released a Digital Market Landscape study empowering the Executive Director to assess competition effectiveness across digital sectors. For Prosus — listed on the JSE and with OLX operating classifieds in South Africa — this regime creates direct compliance exposure. The OIP Guidance Note, while non-binding, signals enforcement priorities and is likely to inform future complaints and investigations against dominant platforms.
