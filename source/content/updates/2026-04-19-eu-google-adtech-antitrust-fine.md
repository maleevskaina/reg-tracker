## EC Fines Google €2.95B for Ad Tech Antitrust Violations

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — relevant to PayU (payments/fintech advertising), OLX (classifieds reliant on digital advertising), and any Prosus portfolio company that depends on programmatic advertising infrastructure
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [TechPolicy.Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/reviewing-european-antitrust-activity-in-2025-and-what-it-all-means-for-2026/)

In September 2025, the European Commission fined Google €2.95 billion for self-preferencing its own ad exchange (AdX) within its ad tech services, distorting competition in the advertising technology market. This is the largest standalone antitrust fine under EU competition rules to date. The Commission found that Google leveraged its dominance across the publisher ad server (DFP), demand-side platform (DV360), and ad exchange (AdX) to foreclose rivals. For Prosus portfolio companies that spend significantly on digital advertising or operate ad-supported platforms, the decision signals potential structural changes in how programmatic advertising markets are organised in Europe, and may eventually lower costs or open access to competing ad tech stacks.
