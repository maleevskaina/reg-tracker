## CMA Opens Investigation Into Just Eat's Restaurant Ratings System

- **Date**: 27 March 2026
- **Region**: UK
- **Company relevance**: iFood (food delivery ratings), OLX (seller review systems)
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [CMA GOV.UK case page](https://www.gov.uk/cma-cases/just-eat-consumer-protection-enforcement-case)

On 27 March 2026, the Competition and Markets Authority opened a consumer protection enforcement case against Just Eat, investigating whether the platform's restaurant and grocer ratings system has inflated certain businesses' star ratings — giving consumers a potentially misleading picture of quality when choosing where to order. The CMA is simultaneously investigating four other businesses — AutoTrader, Feefo (a reviews platform), Dignity (funerals), and Pasta Evangelists — as part of a coordinated crackdown on fake, misleading, and manipulated online reviews.

The CMA is specifically examining three stages of the reviews ecosystem: how reviews are obtained (incentivised or solicited reviews without adequate disclosure), how they are moderated (suppression of negative reviews), and how aggregate ratings are displayed (inflated star scores that misrepresent the full body of feedback).

**Current status:** The CMA has not reached conclusions on whether consumer law has been breached. A progress update is expected in September 2026. If the CMA finds an infringement, it can require Just Eat to change practices and impose fines of up to 10% of global annual turnover under the DMCCA.

**Direct relevance to iFood:** iFood is Brazil's largest food delivery platform and operates restaurant ratings and review systems analogous to Just Eat's. While the CMA investigation is UK-specific, the underlying conduct — inflated ratings, moderation that suppresses negative feedback, or incentivised reviews without adequate disclosure — would attract regulatory attention from Brazil's PROCON, SENACON, or ANPD (if the ratings involve personal data processing) if replicated in Brazil. The CCI could also take an interest given its oversight of e-commerce platform conduct in India.

**Direct relevance to OLX:** OLX operates seller and dealer rating systems across multiple markets. The CMA's parallel AutoTrader investigation directly concerns classifieds-adjacent dealer ratings. OLX should ensure its review and ratings policies clearly disclose how ratings are calculated, how negative reviews are handled, and whether any promotional tools affect aggregate scores.
