## Brazil ANPD Becomes Independent Regulatory Agency

- **Date**: Late 2025 / confirmed Q1 2026
- **Region**: Brazil
- **Company relevance**: All Prosus portfolio companies operating in Brazil — iFood, PayU Brazil, OLX, Brainly, Eruditus
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [IAPP / Montaury](https://iapp.org/news/a/anpd-becomes-regulatory-agency-a-turning-point-for-brazilian-data-protection-compliance)

Provisional Measure No. 1,317/2025 transformed the ANPD (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados) from an executive body under Ministry oversight into an independent regulatory agency — a special autarchy with full technical, administrative, and financial autonomy. The ANPD now sits alongside Anatel (telecoms regulator) and Anvisa (health regulator) as a peer-level federal regulatory authority governed by the rules of Law No. 13,848/2019 on regulatory agencies.

**What this changes:** Previously, the ANPD operated with limited operational independence and staffing. Under the new structure, the ANPD can hire its own specialist technical staff (with tenured civil service status), maintain an independent budget, and conduct enforcement without government direction. Its enforcement toolkit now explicitly includes the power to order business operations to cease, seize goods, and request police assistance in cases of obstruction. The ANPD more than doubles its operational workforce specifically dedicated to data protection supervision.

**Enforcement trajectory:** The ANPD has signalled it will evolve from a consultative approach to active oversight with heavier sanctions. Combined with its published 2026–2027 Priority Themes Map (children's data, targeted advertising, AI), this signals a meaningful escalation in Brazilian data protection enforcement risk for digital platforms. LGPD fines of up to 2% of Brazilian revenue (capped at R$50m per infringement) are now more likely to be pursued.

**For Prosus companies in Brazil:** iFood handles minors' data (food delivery accounts), PayU Brazil holds sensitive payment data, and OLX maintains extensive user profiles — all areas targeted in the ANPD's enforcement priorities. Companies should review LGPD compliance programmes against the heightened enforcement environment, particularly children's data flows and legitimate interest assessments for profiling.
