## UK DUAA Data Protection Provisions In Force — What Changed on 5 February 2026

- **Date**: 5 February 2026 (in force); 19 June 2026 (next commencement wave)
- **Region**: UK
- **Company relevance**: Stack Overflow, Codecademy, OLX UK, PayU UK
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [Kennedy's Law](https://www.kennedyslaw.com/en/thought-leadership/article/2026/the-data-use-and-access-act-2025-commencement-dates-and-planned-guidance-for-2026/)

The UK Data Use and Access Act 2025 received Royal Assent in June 2025. The Commencement No. 6 Regulations (SI 2026/82) brought the bulk of the data protection and privacy provisions of Part 5 of the DUAA into force on 5 February 2026. Organisations with UK users are now subject to the reformed regime.

**What is now in force (since 5 February 2026):**

**Automated decision-making:** The DUAA reforms the UK GDPR's Article 22 automated decision-making provisions. The new framework replaces the blanket prohibition on solely automated decisions with a risk-based, sector-specific approach. Controllers must still provide meaningful information about automated logic and offer human review for significant decisions — but the strict "solely automated" threshold is adjusted to reflect modern AI and algorithmic processing realities. For Prosus companies using AI for credit scoring (PayU), content recommendation (Stack Overflow), or personalised learning paths (Codecademy), this is operationally significant.

**Purpose limitation and legitimate interests:** The Act introduces a "recognised legitimate interests" schedule — a list of specified purposes where controllers can process personal data without conducting the previously required balancing test. This simplifies certain processing activities but is narrower than GDPR's Article 6(1)(f).

**Data subject rights:** Reformed timelines and procedures for responding to Subject Access Requests (SARs), with clearer grounds for refusal and limits on manifestly excessive requests.

**Research and statistical exemptions:** Updated exemptions for academic and statistical research processing, relevant to data-driven product teams and analytics functions at Prosus portfolio companies.

**Children's data protection by design:** New requirements for platforms accessible to children to embed privacy-protective design by default — closely aligned with the existing ICO Children's Code. Codecademy and any platform with under-18 UK users must ensure compliance.

**What is coming on 19 June 2026:**

- New data subject complaint rights and ICO investigation procedures
- Strengthened powers for the ICO to require organisations to undertake audits
- New obligations on controllers regarding the ICO's Memoranda of Understanding with other regulators

**ICO guidance:** The ICO is publishing updated guidance on enforcement procedures (Spring 2026 consultation) and will release updated practical guidance on the new automated decision-making framework and children's data provisions.

**EU adequacy risk:** The DUAA diverges from EU GDPR in the automated decision-making and legitimate interests provisions. The EU Commission is expected to review the UK's adequacy decision (due for renewal) in light of these changes. Loss of adequacy would impose significant compliance costs on all Prosus companies operating dual UK/EU data flows.
