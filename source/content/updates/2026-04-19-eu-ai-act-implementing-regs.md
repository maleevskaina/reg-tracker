## EU AI Act: Commission Adopts First Implementing Regulations on GPAI Models

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: Swiggy, PayU, Codecademy, Eruditus, Stack Overflow — all deploying AI-powered features
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [European Commission / AI Office](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu)

The European Commission's AI Office has adopted the first implementing regulations under the EU AI Act, specifically targeting General-Purpose AI (GPAI) model providers. Under Article 53, providers must maintain technical documentation, comply with EU copyright law, and publish summaries of training data. High-impact GPAI models (above 10²⁵ FLOPs threshold) face additional systemic risk obligations including adversarial testing and incident reporting. The regulations apply to any company deploying GPAI APIs in EU-facing products. For Prosus portfolio companies, this creates a compliance obligation for any product using OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, or similar APIs — including Swiggy's AI delivery estimations, PayU's fraud scoring, and the AI tutoring features of Codecademy and Eruditus. GPAI obligations apply from August 2026; high-impact model rules from the same date.
