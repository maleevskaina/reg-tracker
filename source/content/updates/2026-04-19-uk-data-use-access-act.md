## UK Data Use and Access Act Receives Royal Assent

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: UK
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — all Prosus companies with UK user bases including Stack Overflow, Codecademy, PayU UK, and OLX
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [UK Parliament / ICO](https://www.legislation.gov.uk)

The UK Data Use and Access Act (DUAA) has received Royal Assent, marking the UK's definitive post-Brexit divergence from EU GDPR. The Act introduces a more flexible "recognised legitimate interests" test for data processing, removing the need for a balancing test in certain categories. It creates Smart Data Schemes enabling regulated data portability across sectors (financial services, energy, telecoms), which has implications for PayU and any Prosus company in financial services. The Act establishes a new Digital Verification Services (DVS) trust framework and reforms the ICO into a strengthened Information Commission with greater financial autonomy. Critically, the UK government is asserting that these changes will preserve EU adequacy status — but the Commission has yet to confirm. For Prosus companies maintaining dual UK/EU data flows, the adequacy question is the key risk to monitor through 2026.
