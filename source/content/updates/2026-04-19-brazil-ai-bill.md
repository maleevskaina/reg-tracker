## Brazil AI Bill (PL 2338/2023) Advances in Senate — Vote Expected Mid-2026

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: Brazil
- **Company relevance**: iFood, PayU Brazil, OLX Autos — all deploying AI systems in Brazilian consumer markets
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [Brazilian Senate / IAPP](https://www25.senado.leg.br)

Brazil's AI Bill (PL 2338/2023), authored by Senator Rodrigo Pacheco, has cleared the Senate's Science and Technology Committee and is scheduled for a full floor vote in mid-2026. The Bill takes a risk-based approach modelled on the EU AI Act, classifying AI systems by risk level and imposing increasingly stringent obligations for high-risk applications. Specifically relevant to Prosus portfolio companies: Article 14 designates algorithmic credit scoring (PayU's core product in Brazil) as high-risk, requiring human review of AI-generated credit decisions and mandatory explanation rights. Article 20 requires operators of algorithmic recommendation systems with more than 5 million Brazilian users — potentially iFood — to conduct algorithmic impact assessments every two years. The Bill also prohibits AI-based social scoring systems and creates a national AI authority. The October 2026 elections create uncertainty about timing, but passage before or immediately after the election is expected.
