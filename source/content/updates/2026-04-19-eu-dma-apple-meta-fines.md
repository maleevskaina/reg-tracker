## EU Fines Apple €500M and Meta €200M Under Digital Markets Act

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — affects all digital marketplace and app distribution operators including OLX, Swiggy, and PayU as platform businesses subject to DMA compliance expectations
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [European Commission / CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/10/google-meta-big-tech-6-billion-euros-eu-fine.html)

The European Commission fined Apple €500 million and Meta €200 million in April 2025 for breaching the Digital Markets Act (DMA). Apple was found to have violated its anti-steering obligation by preventing App Store developers from directing consumers to cheaper offers outside the App Store. Meta's "pay or consent" advertising model — which required users to either pay a subscription or surrender personal data — was found non-compliant with the DMA's user-choice requirements. Both companies were given 60 days to comply. This brings cumulative EU Big Tech fines past €7 billion since 2024. For Prosus portfolio companies operating app-based or platform-based businesses in the EU, these decisions set a strong precedent on data monetisation practices and app distribution fairness that will inform compliance strategies.
