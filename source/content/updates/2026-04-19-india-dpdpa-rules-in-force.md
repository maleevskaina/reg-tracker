## India's DPDPA Rules Finalised — Phased Compliance Now Underway

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: India
- **Company relevance**: Swiggy, PayU, OLX India, Brainly, Eruditus — all Prosus/Naspers portfolio companies with significant Indian user bases
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/with-rules-finalized-india-s-dpdpa-takes-force)

India's Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology finalised the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Rules on 13 November 2025, putting India's first comprehensive data protection framework into operation. Implementation is phased: a Data Protection Board is now being established (Phase 1, active); consent manager registration requirements take effect by November 2026 (Phase 2); and full substantive compliance obligations — including explicit consent, purpose limitation, breach notification, and data localisation — become mandatory from May 2027 (Phase 3). Penalties for breaches of security safeguards can reach INR 250 crore (~€27M). For Prosus portfolio companies operating in India — particularly PayU (payments data), Swiggy (user transaction/location data), and edtech platforms — this requires a structured compliance programme to be in place well ahead of the May 2027 deadline. The interaction between DPDPA obligations and CCI data-related investigations adds additional complexity.
