## USPTO Issues Final Guidance: AI Cannot Be Named as Inventor Under US Patent Law

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: US
- **Company relevance**: PayU (fintech patents), Swiggy (logistics/routing IP), Codecademy, Eruditus (edtech patents)
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [US Patent and Trademark Office](https://www.uspto.gov)

The United States Patent and Trademark Office has issued final guidance confirming that only natural persons can be named as inventors under 35 U.S.C. § 101. The guidance resolves uncertainty following the Federal Circuit's *Thaler v. Vidal* decision by setting out a practical "significant contribution" test: a human inventor using an AI tool must have made a non-trivial intellectual contribution to the specific claimed invention — prompting an AI is insufficient alone. The guidance also addresses patent prosecution strategies: applicants must now document their inventive process to demonstrate human contribution in USPTO office actions where AI use is disclosed. For Prosus portfolio companies with patent portfolios — particularly PayU (payment processing methods), Swiggy (optimisation algorithms and route planning), and the edtech platforms — this requires updating internal IP policies to ensure AI-assisted R&D workflows preserve evidence of human inventive contribution. Similar guidance is expected from the EPO and UK IPO in 2026.
