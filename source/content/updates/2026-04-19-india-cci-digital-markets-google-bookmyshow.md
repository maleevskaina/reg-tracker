## CCI Closes Google Play and BookMyShow Cases — Schott Glass Evidentiary Standard Applied

- **Date**: March–April 2026
- **Region**: India
- **Company relevance**: Swiggy (ongoing separate CCI case), PayU India (digital payments platform)
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [Mondaq / Axiom5 Competition Monthly](https://www.mondaq.com/india/antitrust-eu-competition/1772200/competition-monthly-april-2026)

The Competition Commission of India closed two prominent digital platform investigations in March 2026. In the Google Play Store case (closed 24 March 2026), the CCI found no prima facie contravention of Sections 3 or 4 of the Competition Act 2002 — the allegations were that Google had arbitrarily terminated developer accounts on the Play Store without adequate reasons or due process. In the BookMyShow case (closed 12 March 2026), the CCI found no abuse of dominant position despite allegations of exclusive cinema agreements, discriminatory revenue sharing, restrictive data practices, and denial of market access to rival ticketing platforms.

**The Schott Glass standard:** Both closures reflect the Supreme Court's 2025 Schott Glass judgment, which mandated that abuse of dominance findings under Section 4 require evidence of actual or potential anti-competitive effects — not merely proof of dominant position plus conduct. This raises the bar for complainants and encourages the CCI to be more selective in which investigations it pursues to a full DG inquiry.

**What this means for Swiggy:** Swiggy remains subject to a separate CCI investigation (opened in 2024) into alleged anti-competitive practices in food delivery, including mandatory exclusivity requirements with restaurants, algorithmic pricing, and preferential self-listings. The Schott Glass standard does not immunise Swiggy from enforcement, but it does mean the CCI will need to demonstrate actual market harm — not merely Swiggy's market leadership — to sustain a finding. Swiggy should ensure its commercial practices with restaurant partners are defensible on efficiency grounds.

**AI market study:** The CCI has signalled a growing interest in algorithmic pricing, AI-driven personalisation, and data advantages as competition concerns in digital markets. Its ongoing market study on AI is expected to produce findings relevant to PayU (AI-driven credit scoring and fraud detection) and Swiggy (AI-powered recommendation and surge pricing).
