## CMA Issues First DMCCA Consumer Fine: AA Driving Schools, £4.2M for Drip Pricing

- **Date**: 15 April 2026
- **Region**: UK
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — any UK-facing digital platform with checkout flows, subscription pricing, or booking systems
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [CMA / Lewis Silkin](https://www.lewissilkin.com/en/insights/2026/04/15/consumer-law-revolution-cma-issues-first-fine-of-4-2-million-for-consumer-law-b-102mpnr)

On 15 April 2026 the Competition and Markets Authority imposed a £4.2 million fine on AA Driving School and BSM Driving School — the first financial penalty for a substantive consumer law breach using the CMA's new direct enforcement powers under the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 (DMCCA). The CMA found that between April and December 2025, AA/BSM websites showed learner drivers a headline price during the booking flow but only disclosed a mandatory £3 booking fee at the final checkout stage, after customers had already invested time selecting lessons, choosing times, and entering personal details. Over 80,000 customers were affected. The CMA ordered full refunds and imposed a £7 million fine, reduced to £4.2 million (40% reduction) after AA agreed to settle early.

**Why this matters beyond the specific case:** This is not an isolated case. The CMA has open investigations into drip pricing and misleading fees against multiple sectors. Its enforcement strategy 2026–2029 explicitly prioritises online pricing practices. The DMCCA gives the CMA power to fine up to 10% of a company's global annual turnover for consumer law breaches — without going to court. Prior to the DMCCA, the CMA had to seek court orders; enforcement was slow and uncertain. That constraint has been removed.

**Relevance to Prosus portfolio companies:** Any UK-facing digital platform with checkout flows, subscription pricing, service fees, or booking mechanisms needs to audit how costs are disclosed. Mandatory fees must be included in the headline price — not added at the end. This applies to OLX UK dealer subscription tiers, Codecademy subscription pricing flows, PayU payment gateway service fees displayed to UK merchants, and any platform using dark patterns in pricing presentation. The CMA has indicated further cases are imminent.
