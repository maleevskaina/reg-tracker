## CCI Finds Swiggy and Zomato Violated Competition Law — Final Decision Pending

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: India
- **Company relevance**: **Swiggy** (direct Prosus portfolio company)
- **Priority**: CRITICAL
- **Source**: [Digital Watch Observatory](https://dig.watch/updates/indias-antitrust-watchdog-finds-zomato-and-swiggy-violated-competition-laws)

India's Competition Commission (CCI) investigation has found that both Swiggy and Zomato violated competition law by favouring select restaurant partners on their platforms. Swiggy was found to have promised growth incentives to restaurants that listed exclusively with it — a form of exclusivity dealing that the CCI's investigation team concluded restricts competition. Zomato was found to have used exclusivity contracts to offer lower commission rates. The investigation was triggered by a 2022 complaint from the National Restaurant Association of India. As of early 2026, a formal final decision from the CCI remains pending. Separately, a new antitrust complaint has been filed by India's All India Consumer Products Distributors Federation over alleged predatory pricing by Swiggy's quick commerce arm (Instamart), Blinkit, and Zepto. Prosus holds a major stake in Swiggy, making this a direct regulatory exposure with potential fines, operational remedies, and reputational implications.
