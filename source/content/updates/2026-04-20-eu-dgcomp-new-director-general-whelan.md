## Anthony Whelan Appointed as New EU DG COMP Director-General

- **Date**: April 2026
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — EU competition enforcement affects all Prosus companies with EU market presence
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [Cleary Antitrust Watch](https://www.clearyantitrustwatch.com/2026/04/eu-commission-appoints-new-director-general-for-competition/)

The European Commission has appointed Anthony Whelan as the new Director-General of DG COMP, the Commission's competition enforcement directorate, replacing Olivier Guersent who had led the department since 2015. Whelan has served as Deputy Director-General and brings continuity alongside the potential for shifts in enforcement style and prioritisation.

**Timing and context:** The appointment comes amid one of the most active periods in EU competition enforcement history. DG COMP is currently managing:
- The fallout from the €4.34 billion Google AdTech fine (April 2025), subject to appeal at the General Court
- Apple and Meta DMA non-compliance fines (March 2026)
- Active DMA investigations into cloud computing interoperability (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)
- Meta/WhatsApp AI data processing interim measures
- Multiple merger reviews in digital infrastructure and AI sectors

**What to watch:** New Director-Generals typically undertake a review of pending cases and may signal shifts in settlement appetite, fine calculation methodology, or enforcement sequencing. Whelan's background in digital markets enforcement suggests continuity in DMA and AI-related competition work.

**Relevance for Prosus portfolio companies:** Companies in active or anticipated DG COMP proceedings — including those in food delivery (Glovo/Delivery Hero cartel fine precedent), digital payments, and edtech — should monitor whether the new leadership brings changes to the Commission's approach to remedies and settlement negotiations. The DMA enforcement track, which affects app store access and interoperability relevant to Swiggy, Codecademy, and Stack Overflow, is expected to intensify under Whelan.
