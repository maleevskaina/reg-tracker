## Brazil ANPD Sets 2026–2027 Enforcement Priorities: Children's Data and Targeted Advertising in Focus

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: Brazil
- **Company relevance**: iFood, OLX Autos, PayU Brazil — all processing data of Brazilian users potentially including minors
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [Brazil ANPD / IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/notes-from-the-asia-pacific-region-india-releases-dpdpa-rules-ai-governance-guidelines)

Brazil's National Data Protection Authority (ANPD) has approved its Priority Themes Map for 2026–2027, identifying two headline enforcement priorities: (1) **children's and adolescents' data protection**, with active supervision of secondary uses of minor data for targeted advertising; and (2) **targeted advertising practices** more broadly, reflecting their centrality to dominant digital business models. The ANPD currently has active supervisory actions against social media networks and messaging platforms on children's data and consent, with first enforcement decisions expected in 2026. Brazil's Digital Statute of the Child and Adolescent (Digital ECA), effective 17 March 2026, represents the world's most comprehensive child digital protection law: it requires age verification on access to platforms, prohibits behavioural profiling of minors, and mandates privacy-by-design for all children-facing features. Separately, in a significant trade development, the EU and Brazil have reached a mutual adequacy decision, recognising equivalent levels of data protection — simplifying EU-Brazil data flows for all Prosus portfolio companies. The ANPD's increased institutional independence (transformed into a full regulatory agency in 2025) means enforcement is accelerating.
