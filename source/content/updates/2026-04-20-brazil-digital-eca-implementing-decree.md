## Brazil Publishes Digital ECA Implementing Decree — August 2026 Compliance Deadline

- **Date**: 18 March 2026 (published); compliance deadline 2 August 2026
- **Region**: Brazil
- **Company relevance**: Codecademy, Eruditus, iFood, Stack Overflow
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [Baker McKenzie](https://www.bakermckenzie.com/en/insight/publications/2026/03/brazil-regulates-the-children-and-adolescents-online-safety-act)

Decree 12,880/2026 was published on 18 March 2026 and provides the regulatory framework implementing Law No. 15,211/2025, Brazil's Digital Statute for Children and Adolescents (known as the "Digital ECA"). The Digital ECA was enacted in response to growing concern about social media harm to minors in Brazil and imposes obligations broadly analogous to the UK's Age Appropriate Design Code and the US COPPA.

**Key obligations under Decree 12,880/2026:**

**Age verification:** Digital platforms must implement age verification mechanisms to identify users under 18. The decree does not mandate a specific technical standard but requires that verification be reliable and proportionate to the risk of harm.

**Parental supervision tools:** Platforms must provide parents and guardians with tools to monitor and restrict minors' usage, including parental control dashboards, usage time limits, and the ability to disable certain features.

**Algorithmic content restrictions:** Platforms must not use algorithmic recommendation systems to push content to minors that could expose them to violence, self-harm, exploitation, or illegal activities. This includes a duty to audit recommendation models for harmful outputs.

**Advertising prohibition:** Targeted advertising to children under 12 is prohibited. Behavioural advertising to users aged 12–17 requires explicit parental consent.

**Compliance deadline:** 2 August 2026 — exactly one year after the Digital ECA came into force.

**Enforcement:** ANPD (for data protection aspects) and SENACON (for consumer protection aspects) share enforcement jurisdiction. Fines can reach up to 2% of revenue in Brazil, capped at R$50 million per violation.

**Direct relevance to Prosus portfolio companies:**

- **Codecademy**: Operates coding education platforms accessed by students as young as 13. Must audit age verification at registration, ensure no behavioural advertising to under-18s, and review recommendation features for compliance.
- **Eruditus**: While primarily targeting professionals, any Brazilian marketing or platform access by minors requires controls.
- **iFood**: Food delivery platforms are frequently used by teenagers. iFood must assess whether its algorithmic recommendations and promotional tools comply with the advertising restrictions for users under 18.
- **Stack Overflow**: Large Brazilian developer community includes students and minors. Age gate and advertising controls require review.
