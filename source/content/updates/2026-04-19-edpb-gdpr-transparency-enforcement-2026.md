## EDPB Launches 2026 Coordinated GDPR Transparency Enforcement Action Across 25 DPAs

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: All Prosus portfolio companies with EU user bases: OLX, PayU, Stack Overflow, Udemy, Codecademy, GoodHabitz, Eruditus
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [European Data Protection Board](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/news/news/2026/cef-2026-edpb-launches-coordinated-enforcement-action-transparency-and-information_en)

The EDPB's 2025 Annual Report, published on 9 April 2026, confirmed that national DPAs across Europe issued €1.15 billion in GDPR fines in 2025 — with Ireland's DPC alone accounting for €530M, driven primarily by the TikTok cross-border data transfer decision. For 2026, the EDPB has activated its Coordinated Enforcement Framework (CEF) with 25 participating DPAs, focusing specifically on **GDPR transparency and information obligations** — i.e., whether privacy notices clearly inform users about how their data is processed, for what purposes, and with which third parties. This is a direct compliance risk for all Prosus portfolio companies operating consumer-facing digital products in the EU. OLX (classifieds), PayU (payments), Stack Overflow (developer community), and edtech platforms should audit their privacy notices, consent flows, and data processing disclosures against the standard the CEF will apply. Investigations are expected to conclude with formal decisions in late 2026 or 2027.
