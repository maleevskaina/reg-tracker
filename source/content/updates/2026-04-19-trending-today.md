# Trending Today — Digital Regulatory Briefing, 19 April 2026

Broader digital regulatory news relevant to a digital regulatory team tracking global platform law.

---

## 1. EU–US Tech Tensions Escalating Over Big Tech Fines

The Trump administration has threatened retaliatory tariffs of up to 25% on EU imports following the European Commission's accumulated €6B+ in Big Tech fines since 2024. The Commission has signalled it will not reduce enforcement activity in response to trade pressure. This geopolitical friction is relevant to any multinational digital business operating across both jurisdictions — including Prosus/Naspers, which has significant EU and US exposure.

**Source**: [CNBC, 10 April 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/10/google-meta-big-tech-6-billion-euros-eu-fine.html)

---

## 2. Brazil Considering DMA-Style Ex Ante Digital Regulation

Bill PL 4675/2025 proposes a formal ex ante regulatory framework for "systemic" digital platforms in Brazil, modelled loosely on the EU's DMA. It would create a Digital Markets Superintendency within CADE. Passage is unlikely before Brazil's October 2026 elections, but the proposal signals the direction of travel. This is directly relevant to iFood, OLX Autos, and PayU Brazil.

**Source**: [ITIF, February 2026](https://itif.org/publications/2026/02/20/brazil-should-avoid-rushing-into-dma-style-regulation/)

## 3. India's AI Market Regulation Under Scrutiny

A ProMarket analysis published April 2026 flags risks in India's approach to AI market regulation — warning that early frameworks risk being built on outdated theories of harm. The CCI's growing interest in algorithmic pricing, data advantages, and AI-enabled market power will intersect with the DPDPA's data governance rules. Relevant context for Prosus portfolio companies deploying AI features in India (Swiggy recommendations, PayU fraud detection).

**Source**: [ProMarket, 7 April 2026](https://www.promarket.org/2026/04/07/indias-ai-market-regulation-risks-falling-on-dated-ideas/)

## 4. COMESA Regional Competition Focus on Digital Markets

The Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA) Competition Commission is increasing focus on digital markets, with enforcement coordination across member states relevant to OLX and other Prosus companies active in African markets.

**Source**: [Cliffe Dekker Hofmeyr, March 2026](https://www.cliffedekkerhofmeyr.com/en/news/publications/2026/South-Africa/Competition-Law/competition-law-alert-19-march-A-focus-on-digital-markets-in-COMESA)

## 5. EU Antitrust Investigations Now Targeting AI Practices

The European Commission has opened preliminary investigations into whether Google is using web content to train generative AI models without publisher consent, and whether Meta is restricting third-party AI providers on WhatsApp. These AI-specific competition investigations are a new frontier and will shape how all platform companies design their AI product features across the EU.

**Source**: [TechPolicy.Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/reviewing-european-antitrust-activity-in-2025-and-what-it-all-means-for-2026/)
