## EU and Brazil Adopt Mutual Adequacy Decision

- **Date**: 27 January 2026
- **Region**: EU / Brazil
- **Company relevance**: iFood, PayU Brazil, OLX, Eruditus, Codecademy — all companies with active EU–Brazil personal data flows
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [European Commission / IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/brazil-eu-finalize-adequacy-agreement)

On 27 January 2026, the European Commission and Brazil's National Data Protection Authority (ANPD) simultaneously adopted mutual adequacy decisions, confirming that the EU and Brazil each provide an essentially equivalent level of protection for personal data. The EU Commission adopted Implementing Decision (EU) 2026/179 under Article 45 GDPR, while the ANPD adopted Resolution No. 32 under the LGPD. These are the first mutual (bilateral) adequacy decisions ever issued under the GDPR framework — all prior EU adequacy decisions were unilateral.

**What changes for Prosus portfolio companies:** Personal data can now flow from the EU to Brazil and from Brazil to the EU without any additional transfer mechanism. Standard Contractual Clauses, Binding Corporate Rules, or derogation-based justifications are no longer required for those data flows. This simplifies data operations for iFood (EU investor/partner data, EU tech stack), PayU Brazil (EU payment processing linkages), OLX (EU-headquartered platform with Brazilian classifieds users), and edtech platforms with EU teachers/content and Brazilian learners. It also reduces compliance overhead on DPAs (data processing agreements) and DPIAs for cross-border flows.

**Scope and limitations:** The adequacy decisions cover private and public sector data processing. However, they do not apply to transfers made exclusively for national security, defense, or criminal law enforcement purposes. The ANPD will reassess adequacy within four years.

**Operational action:** Any existing SCCs or transfer impact assessments maintained purely for EU–Brazil flows should be reviewed and potentially retired. DPIAs and RoPA entries covering those flows should be updated to reflect the adequacy legal basis.
