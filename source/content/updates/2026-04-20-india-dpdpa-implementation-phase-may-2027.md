## India DPDPA: 13 Months to Full Enforcement — Compliance Implementation Window Now

- **Date**: Rules in force 13 November 2025; full enforcement 12 May 2027; current date: April 2026
- **Region**: India
- **Company relevance**: Swiggy, PayU India, OLX India — highest priority; all Prosus companies with Indian users
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [Latham & Watkins / JDSupra](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/india-s-digital-personal-data-4101201/)

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDPA) and the DPDP Rules 2025 are now in the implementation phase. Procedural provisions and the Data Protection Board of India (DPBI) became operational on 13 November 2025. Substantive obligations on data fiduciaries (controllers) become enforceable from **12 May 2027** — leaving 13 months for compliance build-out.

**What is already operational:**

The Data Protection Board of India (DPBI) — the adjudicatory body for DPDPA complaints and enforcement — is active. The Board can receive complaints from data principals and investigate breaches. The consent manager registration framework comes into force 12 months after the Rules' publication (i.e., November 2026).

**Key obligations that will apply from May 2027:**

**Consent:** Must be free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous. Unlike GDPR's six lawful bases, the DPDPA primarily relies on consent, with narrow "legitimate uses" exceptions (employment necessity, state function, public interest). For Swiggy and PayU, this requires a consent architecture rebuild — particularly for cross-selling, personalisation, and fraud detection workflows currently relying on contractual necessity or legitimate interests.

**Data principal rights:** Right to access, correction, erasure, and grievance redressal. Data fiduciaries must respond within prescribed timelines. Swiggy processes data for tens of millions of Indian users — building a scalable rights management pipeline is a material infrastructure task.

**Breach notification:** Data fiduciaries must notify the DPBI and affected data principals of personal data breaches. The Rules set notification timelines and content requirements.

**Cross-border transfers:** Data can be transferred to "notified countries" on a government-maintained whitelist. The Rules do not yet specify a transfer mechanism equivalent to GDPR SCCs — the whitelist approach means transfers depend on government publication of approved jurisdictions. For Prosus group data flows into the Netherlands (HQ), EU adequacy status for India is not yet established — organisations should prepare SCCs as a contingency.

**Significant data fiduciaries (SDFs):** The government may designate certain data fiduciaries as SDFs based on volume, sensitivity, or national security considerations. SDFs face enhanced obligations including mandatory DPIAs, data audits, and algorithmic accountability requirements. Swiggy and PayU are strong candidates for SDF designation given their scale of Indian personal data processing.

**Penalties:** INR 250–2,500 crore (approximately €27M–€270M) for significant breaches. The DPBI can also impose urgent remedial measures.

**What distinguishes DPDPA from GDPR (relevant for multinationals running dual compliance):**

| Dimension | DPDPA | GDPR |
|---|---|---|
| Lawful bases | Consent + limited legitimate uses | Six bases including legitimate interests |
| Data localisation | No blanket mandate; SDF restrictions possible | No mandate |
| Cross-border transfers | Government whitelist | Adequacy + SCCs + BCRs |
| DPO requirement | Not required | Required for many controllers |
| Extraterritorial reach | Applies to processing of Indian data principals' data outside India | Applies to offering goods/services to EU residents |

**Action for Prosus portfolio companies:** Companies with Indian operations should: (1) complete DPDPA gap analysis against current privacy infrastructure; (2) rebuild consent flows to DPDPA standard; (3) design data principal rights response processes; (4) assess SDF designation risk and prepare for enhanced obligations; (5) establish breach notification protocols. The May 2027 deadline is firm and the DPBI is already operational.
