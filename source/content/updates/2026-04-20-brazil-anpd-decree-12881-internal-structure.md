## Brazil ANPD New Internal Structure — Decree 12,881/2026

- **Date**: 8 April 2026 (effective)
- **Region**: Brazil
- **Company relevance**: OLX Brazil, iFood, PayU Brazil, all Prosus companies with Brazilian user data
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [ANPD Official Website](https://www.gov.br/anpd/pt-br)

Decree 12,881/2026, published in the Official Gazette and effective from 8 April 2026, approves ANPD's new internal organisational structure. This decree is the operational implementation of the independence framework established by Provisional Measure 1317/2025 (which converted ANPD from a body within the Presidency's structure into a fully autonomous special autarchy).

**New structural units of significance:**

**Enforcement Directorate (Diretoria de Fiscalização):** A dedicated enforcement directorate with expanded staffing, responsible for investigating LGPD violations, conducting inspections, and managing the administrative sanction process. Previously, enforcement capacity was limited by ANPD's position within the executive hierarchy.

**International Cooperation Unit:** Dedicated capacity for engaging with other data protection authorities — particularly the EDPB, ICO, and the EU AI Office — on joint investigations and adequacy monitoring. This is directly relevant to the EU-Brazil LGPD/GDPR adequacy process.

**Technology Assessment Team:** A specialist technical team for evaluating AI systems, automated decision-making tools, and novel data processing technologies against LGPD standards.

**What this means in practice:** ANPD has to date issued relatively few formal enforcement decisions. The new structure removes the structural bottlenecks that constrained enforcement and signals Brazil's intent to become a materially active data protection regulator alongside the EDPB and ICO. The combination of ANPD's new independence (from MP 1317/2025), full financial autonomy (from Decree 12,881/2026), and the EU-Brazil adequacy process creates strong incentives for ANPD to demonstrate rigorous enforcement.

**Relevance for Prosus portfolio companies:** Companies that have deferred LGPD compliance on the assumption that ANPD enforcement remained limited should reassess. OLX Brazil (classifieds with large personal data volumes), iFood (food delivery with payment and location data), and PayU Brazil (financial data) are the highest-exposure entities. Data mapping, lawful basis documentation, and data subject rights processes should be reviewed against ANPD's published enforcement priorities for 2026.
