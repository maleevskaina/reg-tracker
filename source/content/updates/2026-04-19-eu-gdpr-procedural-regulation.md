## EU GDPR Procedural Regulation: Key Changes

- **Date**: In force 1 January 2026; applies to new cases from 2 April 2027
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — all Prosus companies with EU data subjects
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [European Commission / McCann FitzGerald](https://www.mccannfitzgerald.com/knowledge/data-privacy-and-cyber-risk/new-regulation-to-streamline-cross-border-gdpr-enforcement)

Regulation (EU) 2025/2518 of 26 November 2025, known as the GDPR Procedural Regulation, entered into force on 1 January 2026. It applies to cross-border GDPR enforcement cases where complaints are lodged with a supervisory authority from 2 April 2027 onwards.

**Key reforms:**

**Binding investigation timelines:** The Lead Supervisory Authority (LSA) must complete cross-border investigations within 15 months, extendable by up to 12 months in particularly complex cases. This directly addresses the years-long delays that have marked Ireland's DPC handling of complaints against Meta, Google, and TikTok.

**Early resolution mechanism:** A fast-track procedure allows DPAs to resolve complaints about Chapter III GDPR rights (access, erasure, portability) without involving other DPAs, provided the alleged infringement has been remedied and the complainant does not object.

**Complainant rights:** Complainants must be given reasons before their complaint is rejected or dismissed, and a 3–6 week window to submit written observations. This strengthens the procedural position of regulators and consumer advocacy groups bringing complaints against digital platforms.

**One-stop-shop cooperation:** The regulation streamlines how the LSA and Concerned Supervisory Authorities (CSAs) cooperate, with clearer timelines for the mutual assistance and joint operations procedures.

**Relevance for Prosus portfolio companies:** The regulation accelerates the GDPR enforcement cycle. Stack Overflow, PayU, OLX, and edtech platforms that process EU user data are subject to this regime. Companies with data processing operations registered in Ireland or Luxembourg (common EU headquarters for tech companies) should anticipate faster enforcement timelines when complaints are lodged from 2027. Privacy notices, consent mechanisms, and data subject rights processes should be reviewed against the EDPB's 2026 coordinated enforcement focus on transparency.
