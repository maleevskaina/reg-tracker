## CMA Issues First Conduct Requirements Under UK SMS Regime for Google and Apple

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: UK
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — directly relevant to OLX, Swiggy, PayU, Udemy, Codecademy, and any Prosus portfolio company distributing apps via Apple App Store or Google Play in the UK
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [Kennedy's Law](https://www.kennedyslaw.com/en/thought-leadership/article/2026/the-uk-digital-markets-regime-in-action-the-cma-s-first-conduct-requirements-for-google-and-commitments-for-apple-and-google-mobile-platforms/)

Under the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 (DMCCA), the CMA designated Google as having Strategic Market Status (SMS) in search and search advertising in October 2025, and both Google and Apple in mobile platforms. In January 2026, the CMA proposed its first Conduct Requirements targeting Google's search services, and by April 2026, commitments from Apple and Google on app store fairness and iOS interoperability took effect. These include improved app store processes, clearer app review criteria, and enhanced interoperability obligations. For Prosus portfolio companies distributing apps in the UK, this creates real leverage to challenge unfair rejection decisions and commission structures — and for app-based businesses like Swiggy, Udemy, or Codecademy, the changes could meaningfully reduce distribution costs and improve discoverability.
