## CMA Opens Consumer Protection Investigation Into AutoTrader Over Suppressed Dealer Reviews

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: UK
- **Company relevance**: **OLX** — classified marketplace with seller/dealer review infrastructure closely analogous to AutoTrader
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [CMA / Motor Trader](https://www.motortrader.com/motor-trader-news/automotive-news/cma-launches-consumer-law-investigation-into-reviews-on-auto-trader-27-03-2026)

The Competition and Markets Authority opened a formal consumer protection investigation into AutoTrader on 26 March 2026, targeting alleged suppression of negative dealer reviews on the platform. The CMA is investigating whether AutoTrader failed to publish 1-star reviews that were moderated by its third-party review partner Feefo, and whether these omissions caused misleading star ratings to be displayed to consumers — an unfair commercial practice under the Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008 and the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024. This is one of five simultaneous fake/misleading reviews investigations launched by the CMA under its new direct enforcement powers (no court required). The CMA can now impose fines directly of up to 10% of global turnover without litigation. An update is expected in September 2026. For Prosus, this case is directly relevant to **OLX's** classified marketplace operations: OLX relies on seller reputation scores and review systems to drive trust. The CMA's precedent establishes that suppressing or selectively displaying user-generated reviews — even via a third-party moderation partner — constitutes a breach of consumer protection law. OLX should audit its review moderation processes for UK compliance.
