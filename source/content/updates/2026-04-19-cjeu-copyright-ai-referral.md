## CJEU Accepts Copyright and AI Training Referral — Text and Data Mining Exception at Issue

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: Stack Overflow, Codecademy, Eruditus — AI content and learning platforms with user-generated content
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [CJEU / Kluwer IP Law](https://curia.europa.eu)

The Court of Justice of the EU has accepted a copyright referral from a German court asking whether AI training using web-scraped content qualifies as text and data mining (TDM) under Article 4 of the Digital Single Market (DSM) Directive. The referral specifically asks whether the opt-out mechanism for rights holders — which requires machine-readable reservation notices — is compatible with fundamental rights under the EU Charter. A secondary question asks whether an AI model's output that reproduces protected expression constitutes infringement even where the training input qualified for the TDM exception. The case is likely to take 18–24 months to resolve. For Prosus, Stack Overflow is directly affected: its public Q&A content has been widely used to train coding AI models, and the platform has an active licensing programme for this data. The ruling will set the baseline for AI content licensing obligations across Europe.
