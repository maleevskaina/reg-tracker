## CJEU Clarifies SEP/FRAND Licensing Obligations in Landmark Interdigital Ruling

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: PayU, OLX — technology hardware procurement and connectivity licensing
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [Court of Justice of the European Union](https://curia.europa.eu)

The Court of Justice of the EU has handed down a preliminary ruling clarifying the obligations of standard-essential patent (SEP) holders when licensing on FRAND (fair, reasonable, and non-discriminatory) terms. The ruling confirms that an SEP holder abuses a dominant position under Article 102 TFEU if it initiates injunction proceedings before making a genuine FRAND offer, and sets out a step-by-step procedural framework for both parties in licensing negotiations. The Court also confirmed that national courts must stay injunction proceedings if a willing licensee demonstrates it is negotiating in good faith. For Prosus portfolio companies procuring technology infrastructure — including payment terminals (PayU), connected devices, and any 5G-enabled services — the ruling clarifies rights when licensing 5G, Wi-Fi 6, or AVC/HEVC video codec patents. The European Commission's separate SEP Regulation remains under discussion and will interact with this ruling.
