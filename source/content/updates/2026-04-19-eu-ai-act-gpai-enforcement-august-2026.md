## EU AI Act: GPAI Enforcement Powers Activate 2 August 2026

- **Date**: Enforcement powers take effect 2 August 2026
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: Swiggy, PayU, Codecademy, Eruditus, Stack Overflow, iFood — all with AI-powered product features
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [EU AI Office / Latham & Watkins](https://www.lw.com/en/insights/eu-ai-act-gpai-model-obligations-in-force-and-final-gpai-act-gpai-code-of-practice-in-place)

Since 2 August 2025, general-purpose AI model providers have been subject to transparency, copyright compliance, and systemic risk management obligations under Chapter V of the EU AI Act. However, the Commission's active enforcement and supervision powers — the ability to impose fines — were held back for one year. That transition period ends on **2 August 2026**.

**What activates on 2 August 2026:**
- The AI Office's power to request documentation and technical information from GPAI model providers
- The power to conduct technical evaluations and capability assessments
- The power to require compliance measures or market restriction, recall, or withdrawal
- The power to **impose fines** of up to 3% of worldwide annual turnover or €15 million (whichever is higher) for non-compliance

**Who is a GPAI model provider?** Any organisation that trains or fine-tunes a general-purpose AI model and makes it available in the EU, including via API or as an integrated product feature. Notably, GPAI obligations apply to model providers — not merely to deployers using third-party models (e.g. via OpenAI or Anthropic APIs). Prosus portfolio companies that have developed proprietary AI models (e.g. Swiggy's recommendation system, PayU's fraud detection model) should assess whether those models qualify as GPAI under the Act's definition.

**Code of Practice:** The AI Office published a final GPAI Code of Practice on 10 July 2025. Adherence creates a presumption of conformity with GPAI obligations. Companies deploying commercially available GPAI models from providers who have signed the Code benefit from downstream compliance simplification.

**Grace period for legacy models:** Providers of GPAI models released before 2 August 2025 have until 2 August 2027 to achieve full compliance — but active enforcement against new models begins in 107 days.

**Action required:** All Prosus portfolio companies using or developing AI systems in the EU should (1) map which AI features may constitute or rely on GPAI models, (2) confirm that model providers are AI Act compliant, (3) review internal transparency documentation for AI-powered features used in the EU, and (4) ensure copyright compliance processes are in place for any training data used in the EU.
