## CMA 2026–27 Annual Plan: Key Enforcement Priorities

- **Date**: 8 April 2026
- **Region**: UK
- **Company relevance**: OLX UK, Codecademy, PayU, Stack Overflow — subscription and pricing practices; all UK-facing digital platforms
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [Cooley / CMA](https://www.cooley.com/news/insight/2026/2026-04-08-uk-competition-and-consumer-regulator-sets-key-regulatory-priorities-for-2026-2027)

The Competition and Markets Authority published its Annual Plan for 2026–27 on 8 April 2026. The plan identifies five strategic priorities and signals where enforcement resources and new regulatory powers will be concentrated over the coming year.

**1. Subscription contract rules — commencement in 2026**

New rules on subscription contracts under the DMCCA are expected to come into force during 2026. These will require businesses offering recurring subscription services to:
- Provide clear pre-contract information about recurring charges and cancellation rights
- Send reminder notices before free trials convert to paid subscriptions
- Provide simple cancellation mechanisms ("easy exit")
- Offer pro-rata refunds in certain circumstances

Any UK-facing platform with a subscription model — including Codecademy (coding courses), OLX (dealer subscriptions), Stack Overflow (Teams/Enterprise tiers), and PayU (merchant SaaS fees) — needs to audit subscription flows against these incoming rules before commencement.

**2. AI and algorithmic collusion**

The CMA plans to invest significantly in capability to detect algorithmic pricing collusion. The concern is that competing platforms using the same third-party AI pricing tools could converge on prices without human instruction — constituting tacit collusion under competition law. The CMA is working with academic institutions and international competition agencies on detection methodology.

Swiggy's surge pricing algorithms and iFood's dynamic pricing and promotional tools could attract scrutiny if deployed in UK or EU markets.

**3. Strategic Market Status and conduct requirements**

The CMA is advancing SMS designations under the DMCCA's Digital Markets regime. Following Google and Apple's designations (confirmed in early 2026), the CMA is expected to finalise conduct requirements — including interoperability, self-preferencing restrictions, and data portability obligations — during 2026–27. These will directly affect app store access economics for Prosus portfolio apps.

**4. Consumer enforcement wave**

Following the first DMCCA consumer fine (AA Driving Schools, £4.2M, April 2026), the CMA has signalled further consumer enforcement cases are "imminent." Priority sectors include: subscription billing, drip pricing, fake and manipulated reviews, and misleading green claims.

**5. Mergers in AI and digital infrastructure**

The CMA will scrutinise AI acquisitions and investments closely, including partial acquisitions that confer material influence. Any Prosus M&A activity in AI-adjacent sectors in the UK should be screened for merger control notification requirements.
