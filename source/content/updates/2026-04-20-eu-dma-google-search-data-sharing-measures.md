## EU Commission Proposes Binding Google Search Data-Sharing Measures Under DMA

- **Date**: 16 April 2026 (proposed measures); consultation closes 1 May 2026; final decision by 27 July 2026
- **Region**: EU
- **Company relevance**: Stack Overflow, Codecademy — competitive implications of search data access for AI-powered platforms
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [European Commission DMA Portal](https://digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu/commission-proposes-measures-google-sharing-search-engine-data-third-parties-under-digital-markets-2026-04-16_en)

On 16 April 2026 the European Commission sent its preliminary findings and proposed measures to Alphabet (Google), setting out in binding detail the steps Google must take to comply with its obligation under Article 6(11) of the Digital Markets Act to share search engine data with third parties.

**What the proposed measures require:**

Under the proposed measures, Google must allow third-party search engines — and, critically, AI chatbots with search functionalities — to access search data on fair, reasonable, and non-discriminatory (FRAND) terms. The data to be shared includes:

- **Ranking data**: signals and weights used in Google's search ranking algorithm
- **Query data**: anonymised search queries processed through Google Search
- **Click data**: user click and engagement signals on search results pages
- **View data**: impression and visibility data for organic and paid search results

Paid search result interaction and ranking data is included — not individual paid URLs, but block-level advertising performance data.

**AI chatbots expressly in scope:** By explicitly naming AI chatbots with search functionalities in the proposed measures, the Commission is treating conversational AI systems that answer queries directly as competing in the same market as traditional search engines. This significantly expands the scope beyond search competitors like Bing and DuckDuckGo to cover systems such as ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, and comparable tools.

**29-page technical specification:** The Commission has published a detailed specification document covering:
- Field-level definitions of the data to be shared
- Anonymisation and privacy standards that data must meet before transfer
- Pricing methodology and FRAND rate determination
- Audit and verification regimes for compliance monitoring

**Timeline:**
- Public consultation: open until **1 May 2026**
- Commission may revise measures based on consultation feedback
- Final binding decision expected by: **27 July 2026**

**Relevance for Stack Overflow and other AI-powered platforms:** If adopted, these measures would give search engine alternatives and AI systems access to Google's query and ranking corpus. Stack Overflow, which uses AI to generate and surface answers (including via its GenAI features), competes in the same information-retrieval space. Easier access to search signal data could benefit AI search alternatives and accelerate competition with traditional search-adjacent products. Companies should monitor the final decision for data access licensing implications.
