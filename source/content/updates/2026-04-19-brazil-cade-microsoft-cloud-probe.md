## CADE Opens Antitrust Probe into Microsoft Cloud Licensing in Brazil

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: Brazil
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — Prosus and its portfolio companies (including Stack Overflow, Codecademy, PayU, and iFood) that use Microsoft cloud infrastructure or compete in adjacent SaaS markets in Brazil
- **Priority**: MEDIUM
- **Source**: [Global Competition Review](https://globalcompetitionreview.com/article/cade-launches-fresh-probe-microsofts-cloud-practices)

Brazil's antitrust authority CADE launched an Administrative Inquiry into Microsoft's cloud computing and software licensing practices at the start of 2026, mirroring findings from the UK CMA that Microsoft's licensing terms disadvantage rivals including AWS and Google. The probe focuses on whether Microsoft's bundling of Teams with Office 365, restrictive licensing for running Microsoft software on competing cloud providers, and anti-competitive switching costs constitute abuse of dominance under Brazilian competition law. Brazil is a critical market for Prosus (via iFood, PayU, and OLX Autos), and cloud infrastructure costs affect competitive economics across those businesses. A CADE finding could unlock more favourable cloud pricing and contractual terms. A broader digital platform bill (PL 4675/2025) proposing ex ante regulation of systemic platforms is before Congress, though passage in 2026 remains unlikely ahead of October elections.
