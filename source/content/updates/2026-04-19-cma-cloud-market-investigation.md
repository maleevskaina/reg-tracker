## CMA Cloud Services Market Investigation: Provisional Findings Signal Structural Remedies

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Region**: UK
- **Company relevance**: Sector-wide — Stack Overflow, Swiggy, PayU, iFood, Codecademy, and all cloud-dependent Prosus portfolio companies
- **Priority**: HIGH
- **Source**: [Competition and Markets Authority](https://www.gov.uk/cma)

The CMA's cloud services market investigation has published provisional findings that identify three adverse effects on competition: switching barriers created by proprietary data formats and APIs; egress fees that make it prohibitively expensive to move data between cloud providers; and committed spend discounting practices that lock customers into multi-year contracts with a single provider. The CMA's provisional remedies include mandatory technical interoperability standards to enable data portability, a regulated cap on egress fees at cost, and restrictions on volume-based committed spend programmes. Final remedies are expected in Q3 2026. For Prosus and its portfolio companies — most of which rely heavily on AWS, Azure, or GCP for their infrastructure — the remedies would meaningfully reduce infrastructure costs and improve bargaining power in cloud procurement. Stack Overflow's Developer Survey consistently shows heavy AWS dependence across the developer community. If adopted, these remedies would also align with parallel investigations by the EU Commission and CADE (Brazil).
